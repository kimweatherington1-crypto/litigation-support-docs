# Deposition Coordination Workflow
**Kim Weatherington | Legal Operations Portfolio**

---

## Purpose
This workflow governs the end-to-end coordination of depositions — from scheduling through transcript receipt and filing. Proper execution ensures depositions are conducted efficiently, all required documents are served correctly, and the record is preserved without gaps.

---

## Phase 1 — Scheduling (21+ Days Before Deposition)

- [ ] Confirm deponent name, role, and relationship to matter
- [ ] Coordinate availability with all counsel and deponent (or deponent's counsel)
- [ ] Reserve date, time, and location (in-person, remote via Zoom/Teams, or hybrid)
- [ ] Book certified court reporter — confirm availability and rate
- [ ] Book videographer if video deposition is required
- [ ] Reserve conference room or virtual platform link
- [ ] Confirm interpreter if required
- [ ] Enter deposition date in matter calendar with reminders at 14 days, 7 days, and day before

---

## Phase 2 — Subpoena & Notice Preparation

### For Party Witnesses
- [ ] Prepare Notice of Deposition — include date, time, location, and deponent name
- [ ] Serve Notice per applicable rules (federal or state) and calendar service deadline
- [ ] File proof of service

### For Non-Party / Third-Party Witnesses
- [ ] Prepare Subpoena (deposition testimony and/or duces tecum)
- [ ] Identify correct court and obtain signed subpoena from clerk
- [ ] Arrange personal service by process server — confirm service and obtain proof
- [ ] Calendar witness fee payment if required
- [ ] File proof of service

### Deposition Duces Tecum (Document Production)
- [ ] Draft document request schedule — specific, clearly defined categories
- [ ] Confirm production deadline (must allow adequate review time before deposition)
- [ ] Track documents received — log date, format, and completeness
- [ ] Follow up on outstanding production at least 5 days before deposition
- [ ] Organize and Bates-stamp produced documents for use as exhibits

---

## Phase 3 — Preparation (7 Days Before Deposition)

- [ ] Compile exhibit binder — organize chronologically or by topic
- [ ] Prepare exhibit index with Bates numbers
- [ ] Prepare copies of exhibits: original + copies for all counsel + court reporter
- [ ] Confirm logistics with court reporter (exhibit handling, real-time feed if needed)
- [ ] Confirm remote platform access and test technology (if virtual deposition)
- [ ] Prepare deponent file for attorney review:
  - [ ] Prior deposition transcripts
  - [ ] Key documents and correspondence
  - [ ] Chronology of events
  - [ ] Deponent background and prior statements
- [ ] Confirm all parties have received Notice / Subpoena and confirm attendance

---

## Phase 4 — Day of Deposition

- [ ] Arrive / log in 30 minutes early to set up
- [ ] Confirm court reporter present and on record
- [ ] Distribute exhibits to all parties
- [ ] Maintain exhibit log — track all exhibits marked during deposition
- [ ] Monitor for any issues: objections, privilege assertions, breaks
- [ ] Confirm read-and-sign waiver or request for transcript review with court reporter
- [ ] Collect all copies of exhibits at conclusion

## Phase 5 — Post-Deposition (Within 5 Business Days)

- [ ] Order certified transcript from court reporter — confirm delivery deadline
- [ ] Order video recording copy if applicable
- [ ] Upload transcript and exhibits to matter file upon receipt
- [ ] Prepare deposition summary memo for attorney (key testimony, admissions, conflicts)
- [ ] Update case chronology with deposition testimony
- [ ] Calendar errata sheet deadline if read-and-sign was requested
- [ ] Review transcript for confidential information — apply protective order designations if required
